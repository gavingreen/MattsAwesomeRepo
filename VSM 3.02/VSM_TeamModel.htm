Introduction

This document provides a description of an activity-based team model for EMC Solution Methods, with recommended roles and responsibilities for accomplishing a successful EMC project. (Note that he roles in the model do not directly correlate to job titles in EMC Professional Services (EPS).)

This model should be used as a starting point for a project and then tailored to reflect the specific team members needed to successfully complete the project. The same person will often fill multiple roles, but the roles have been separated in this document to delineate the unique responsibilities of each role. The size of the project team will vary depending on the complexity of the work to be performed and the number of people available. Additional roles may also be needed depending on the client and the unique requirements of the project. Responsibilities may shift among the various roles, depending on the needs of the project and the people who are available to fill the roles.

This document does not address post-implementation roles such as production support, user training, conversion, or business process and organizational role changes.

A small project team with only three members might consist of:

·         One person filling the Project Manager, Client Relationship Manager, and Technical Architect roles

·         One person filling the Business Analyst, Technical Lead, and Developer roles

·         One person filling the Business Architect, Documentation and Training Developer, and Quality Assurance roles

If you need to combine two roles that are possibly conflicting, consider options like having developers perform QA on each other’s code rather than on their own code. A small team might also bring in a consultant who is not a full-time team member to review the technical architecture (fill the TA role) or develop the documentation (fill the DT role).

Role Descriptions

The descriptions that follow show the roles that must be filled and the project tasks that need to be performed as part of every web-based development project.

Many of the responsibilities assigned to a particular role could be carried out equally well by a EPS or partner consultant or by a client person. However, in a consulting engagement certain responsibilities must be carried out, reviewed, or supported by EPS or a consulting partner; and certain responsibilities must be carried out by a client person.

The following codes are used in the role descriptions to indicate responsibilities for which there are special requirements in a consulting engagement:

V

Indicates that the responsibility listed should be carried out by EPS or consulting partner personnel. In the case where a client or partner is performing the role under which such a responsibility is listed, this particular responsibility should be shifted to a EPS or consulting partner team member.

R

Indicates that the responsibility listed for that role should be reviewed by EPS or consulting partner personnel. In the case where a client or partner is performing the overall role listed, a EPS or consulting partner team member should have the responsibility for reviewing the deliverables to ensure that EMC’s interests are addressed.

S

Indicates that EPS or consulting partner personnel should help support this responsibility during development so that the person who will later be carrying out the responsibility in production will be better prepared to do so independently.

C

Indicates that the responsibility must be carried out by a client.

·       Keeps the Technical Lead informed of work status

·       Works with the Documentation and Training Developer to communicate information about how the modules and sub-applications work

 

·       Reviews the documentation and training materials for technical accuracy and completeness

V

R

·       Works with Quality Assurance to ensure a successful handover of all modules and work associated with the developed application to the System Administrator for migration to the production environment

Quality Assurance (QA)

Owns the system test planning, execution, and verification process.

Responsibilities

 

·       Works with the Project Manager to develop a project plan and schedule for the quality assurance and testing effort

 

·       Develops the test plan conditions, data, use cases, and expected results for the system test/end user test of the application

 

·       Develops the test cycle and execution plan of when different functionality will be tested

 

·       Coordinates the execution of the system test/end user test

 

·       Documents the test results and any issues from actual results not matching expected results

R

·       Tracks problems/issues encountered during the test execution; verifying that they are documented and then corrected prior to the completion of the test

 

·       Maintains the requirements traceability matrix from a test perspective

R

·       Coordinates regression testing due to change requests and coordinates the re-testing of problems that were encountered during initial testing cycles and have since been corrected

R

·       Coordinates the handover of all modules and work associated with the developed application to the System Administrator for migration to the production environment

Change Management Coordinator (CM)

Coordinates all tasks associated with transitioning to a new organizational structure.

Responsibilities:

C

·       Works with the business units and Business Architect to identify the business process changes needed

C

·       Designs the organizational infrastructure necessary to support the business process changes

C

·       Defines the roles needed in the new organizational structure and manages the organizational change process

C

·       Determines the skills needed for each role and performs a gap analysis between the roles/skills in the current organization versus what is needed in the new organization

C

·       Identifies methods for employees to learn their new responsibilities and increase their proficiency levels

C

·       Creates the transition plan to move from the old organizational structure to the new structure
